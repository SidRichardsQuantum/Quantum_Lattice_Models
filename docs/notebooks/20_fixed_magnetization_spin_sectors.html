Fixed-Magnetization Spin Sectors¶

Model. This notebook constructs XXZ chains directly in fixed total Pauli-$Z$ magnetization sectors and compares the reduced basis with the full computational basis.

Typical uses. Reducing exact-diagonalization cost, checking conserved quantities, validating reduced spectra against full-space blocks, and computing observables or entanglement without embedding reduced states.

Parameters. n_sites controls the full dimension $2^N$; magnetization selects basis states satisfying $\sum_i Z_i=M$; coupling, anisotropy, field, and boundary conditions retain their XXZ meanings.

Useful plots. Dimension scaling shows the sector reduction, while site magnetization and connected correlations characterize a reduced-basis ground state.

sizes = np.arange(4, 15, 2)
full_dimensions = 2**sizes
sector_dimensions = np.array([comb(int(size), int(size // 2)) for size in sizes])

print("sites | full dimension | M=0 dimension | reduction")
print("--- | --- | --- | ---")
for size, full, sector in zip(sizes, full_dimensions, sector_dimensions, strict=True):
    print(f"{size:>5d} | {full:>14d} | {sector:>13d} | {full / sector:>8.2f}x")

    magnetization=0,

    anisotropy=0.7,

    n_sites=8,
    coupling=1.0,
    anisotropy=0.7,
    field=0.15,

states = np.asarray(sector.basis.states)
full_block = full[states][:, states]

print("Sector validation")
print(f"  sector shape: {sector.shape}")
print(f"  block matrices agree: {np.allclose(sector.matrix.toarray(), full_block.toarray())}")

energy, state = ground_state(sector.matrix)
magnetization = site_magnetization_z(state, 8, basis=sector.basis)
correlations = spin_correlation_matrix(
    state,
    8,
    axis="Z",
    basis=sector.basis,
    connected=True,
)
entropy = bipartite_entanglement_entropy(
    state,
    8,
    subsystem=range(4),
    basis=sector.basis,
)

print("Reduced-basis ground state")
print(f"  energy: {energy:.6f}")
print(f"  total magnetization: {magnetization.sum():.6f}")
print(f"  half-chain entropy: {entropy:.6f} bits")
