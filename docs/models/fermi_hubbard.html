Fermi-Hubbard Chain

Purpose and Hamiltonian

The spinful Fermi-Hubbard chain is

$$ H=-t\sum_{\langle i,j\rangle,\sigma} (c_{i\sigma}^\dagger c_{j\sigma}+\mathrm{h.c.}) +U\sum_i n_{i\uparrow}n_{i\downarrow} -\mu\sum_{i,\sigma}n_{i\sigma}. $$

The orbital order is $(0\uparrow,0\downarrow,1\uparrow,1\downarrow,\ldots)$.

Basis and scaling

There are $2N$ binary orbitals and dimension $2^{2N}$. Dense and CSR builders are available.

Package use

from quantum_lattice_models import fermi_hubbard_chain_sparse

H = fermi_hubbard_chain_sparse(n_sites=4, hopping=1.0, interaction=4.0)

Validation and cautions

Single-site energies and an explicit fermionic parity-sign case are tested. The full occupation basis grows faster than the spin-chain basis.
